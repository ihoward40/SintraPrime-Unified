## The 9 Money Documents

### 1. Retail Installment Sale Contract

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| **Status** | ❌ Missing |
| **Why it matters** | Establishes original loan terms, APR, total sale price, payment schedule, finance charges, and the creditor's security interest. Without it, the claimed $12,517.55 balance cannot be independently verified. |
| **Source** | Vroom account online / email archives / UACC records request |
| **What to look for** | Signed contract showing amount financed, finance charge, APR, total of payments, payment schedule, grace period, default terms, repossession rights, and choice of law |
| **Key verification points** | Verify the amount financed matches the vehicle price. Verify APR is correctly stated. Verify default and repossession clauses comply with state law. |

---

### 2. Assignment Chain (Dealer/Vroom → UACC)

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| **Status** | ❌ Missing |
| **Why it matters** | Proves UACC's legal right to enforce the contract and report the debt. If the assignment is defective or UACC cannot prove chain of title, their authority to collect or report is compromised. |
| **Source** | UACC records request / Vroom records |
| **What to look for** | Assignment of retail installment contract from Vroom (or originating dealer) to UACC. Must show effective date, consideration, and transfer of all rights. |
| **Key verification points** | Verify UACC is the current holder. Check for multiple assignments (dealer → intermediary → UACC). Each assignment must be documented. |

---

### 3. Payment History / Life-of-Loan Ledger

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| **Status** | ❌ Missing |
| **Why it matters** | Establishes default date, charge-off date, payment amounts, late fees, and any force-placed insurance or add-on charges. The single most important document for FCRA accuracy disputes. |
| **Source** | UACC written request (FCRA § 623) |
| **What to look for** | Complete transaction history from account opening through charge-off: all payments received (date, amount, method), all fees assessed (late fees, NSF, force-placed insurance), all interest charges, date of last payment, date of default, date of charge-off |
| **Key verification points** | Cross-reference payment dates against bank statements. Verify fee amounts against contract terms. Verify default date matches contract cure provisions. |

---

### 4. Notice of Default / Right to Cure

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| **Status** | ❌ Missing |
| **Why it matters** | Many states require a notice of default and opportunity to cure before repossession. Missing or defective notice can bar deficiency claims and create FDCPA/UCC claims. |
| **Source** | UACC records request / mail records |
| **What to look for** | Written notice stating: amount past due, date by which payment must be made to cure, that failure to cure may result in repossession, and any right to reinstate after repossession |
| **Key verification points** | Verify notice was sent to correct address. Verify cure period complies with state law (typically 10-30 days). Verify notice includes all required disclosures. |

---

### 5. Repossession Notice (UCC § 9-611)

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| **Status** | ❌ Missing |
| **Why it matters** | UCC Article 9 requires "reasonable authenticated notification" before repossession. The notice must describe the debtor's rights and the disposition process. Defective notice is a UCC Article 9 violation and can bar deficiency claims. |
| **Source** | UACC records request / repossession agent / mail records |
| **What to look for** | Notice of intent to repossess, stating: the debtor's right to cure, the amount required to cure, the deadline to cure, and that failure to cure will result in repossession and sale of collateral |
| **Key verification points** | Verify timing (must be sent before repossession). Verify content meets UCC § 9-611 requirements. Verify method of delivery (certified mail preferred). |

---

### 6. Notice of Intent to Sell Collateral (UCC § 9-612/9-613)

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| **Status** | ❌ Missing |
| **Why it matters** | Before selling repossessed collateral, the creditor must notify the debtor of the time, place, and method of sale. The notice must be "commercially reasonable." Defective notice can bar deficiency claims. |
| **Source** | UACC records request / mail records |
| **What to look for** | Notice stating: date, time, and place of sale; method of sale (public auction or private sale); that the debtor may be liable for any deficiency; and the debtor's right to redeem the collateral before sale |
| **Key verification points** | Verify timing (must be sent at least 10 days before sale in most states). Verify content meets UCC § 9-613/9-614 requirements. Verify sale was conducted in a commercially reasonable manner. |

---

### 7. Auction / Private Sale Records

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| **Status** | ❌ Missing |
| **Why it matters** | The sale of repossessed collateral must be "commercially reasonable." Sale records prove (or disprove) whether the vehicle was sold at fair market value. Below-market sales inflate the claimed deficiency. |
| **Source** | UACC records request / auction house / Manheim / ADESA |
| **What to look for** | Auction sheet or private sale record showing: date of sale, sale price, buyer identity, vehicle condition report, odometer reading, and any bids received |
| **Key verification points** | Compare sale price to NADA/KBB/Manheim market value at time of sale. Check for below-market sale to a related party. Verify auction was properly noticed and conducted. |

---

### 8. Post-Sale Deficiency Calculation

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| **Status** | ❌ Missing |
| **Why it matters** | After sale, the creditor must apply proceeds to the debt and provide an accounting. The deficiency is the remaining balance. Improper calculation (including unearned interest, improper fees, or below-market sale) inflates the deficiency. |
| **Source** | UACC records request |
| **What to look for** | Itemized accounting showing: gross sale proceeds, deductions for sale costs (towing, storage, auction fees, attorney fees), application to principal and interest, and resulting deficiency balance |
| **Key verification points** | Verify sale costs are reasonable and documented. Verify interest calculation stops at sale date (not beyond). Verify no force-placed insurance or improper add-ons are included. |

---

### 9. Final Charge-Off / Accounting Ledger

| Field | Value |
|---|---|
| **Status** | ❌ Missing |
| **Why it matters** | The charge-off document shows the final balance UACC wrote off as a loss. This should match the deficiency claimed. Discrepancies between charge-off amount, deficiency claimed, and amount reported to bureaus create FCRA § 623(a) accuracy claims. |
| **Source** | UACC records request / credit reports |
| **What to look for** | Charge-off notice showing: date of charge-off, charge-off amount, any recoveries, and whether a 1099-C was issued |
| **Key verification points** | Verify charge-off amount matches deficiency calculation. Verify charge-off date is accurately reported to bureaus. Check for 1099-C issuance (if issued, debt may be forgiven and no longer collectible). |
