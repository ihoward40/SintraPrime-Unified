## CFPB Complaint Allegation Framework

When the evidence packet is ready, the CFPB complaint should allege:

### Allegation 1: Inaccurate Credit Reporting (FCRA § 623(a))

**Required evidence:** A1, A3, A5, D1-D5
**Theory:** UACC is reporting a $12,517.55 balance in collections without providing documentation that the balance is accurate, the default was properly calculated, or the deficiency was properly determined after repossession and sale.

### Allegation 2: Failure to Conduct Reasonable Reinvestigation (FCRA § 611)

**Required evidence:** C3, C4, C6, D1-D5
**Theory:** If prior disputes were filed, the bureaus failed to conduct a reasonable reinvestigation or UACC failed to respond accurately to ACDV requests.

### Allegation 3: Defective Repossession Notice (UCC Article 9)

**Required evidence:** B1-B5
**Theory:** UACC failed to provide proper notice before or after repossession, or conducted the sale in a commercially unreasonable manner, inflating the claimed deficiency.

### Allegation 4: Failure to Report Disputed Status (FCRA § 623(a)(5))

**Required evidence:** C1, C2, C3, D1-D5
**Theory:** After being notified of the dispute (via CFPB complaint or direct dispute), UACC failed to report the account as disputed to the credit bureaus.
