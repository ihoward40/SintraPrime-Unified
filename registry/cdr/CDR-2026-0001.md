## Question

Should the Blackstone Ecosystem adopt the five-volume governance library as its authoritative knowledge governance framework?

---

## Decision

**YES.** The Blackstone Ecosystem adopts the following five-volume library as the authoritative governance framework for knowledge evaluation, classification, preservation, and decision support:

1. **Volume I — Blackstone Knowledge Governance Constitution (BKGC)**  
   File: `docs/governance/blackstone/BKGC-VOLUME-I.md`

2. **Volume II — Blackstone Governance Standards (BGS)**  
   File: `docs/governance/blackstone/BGS-VOLUME-II.md`

3. **Volume III — Blackstone Reference Architecture (BRA)**  
   File: `docs/governance/blackstone/BRA-VOLUME-III.md`

4. **Volume IV — Blackstone Certification & Compliance Manual (BCCM)**  
   File: `docs/governance/blackstone/BCCM-VOLUME-IV.md`

5. **Volume V — Blackstone Knowledge Registry (BKR)**  
   File: `docs/governance/blackstone/BKR-VOLUME-V.md`

---

## Reasoning

The ecosystem requires a stable, auditable, and intellectually open governance framework that can outlast any particular implementation, model, or source category. The five-volume library separates constitutional principles from operational standards, technical architecture, compliance testing, and canonical definitions. This separation mirrors mature governance models in software engineering and standards organizations.

Key principles ratified:

- **No origin privilege.** Sources are evaluated by evidence, provenance, and jurisdiction, not by whether they are governmental, academic, commercial, or private.
- **Methodological openness.** Minority, unconventional, historical, and privately published materials are legitimate research inputs whose legal status is made explicit.
- **Separation of knowledge governance from decision governance.** Research and evidence evaluation are distinct from operational recommendations.
- **Process compliance, not viewpoint compliance.** The Constitutional Compliance Score measures adherence to governance process, not outcome alignment.
- **Implementation independence.** The Constitution and Standards do not prescribe specific technologies, allowing Hermes, Blackstone, SintraPrime-Unified, Mission Control, and future systems to evolve independently.

## Alternatives Considered

1. **Single governance document.** Rejected because it would mix constitutional principles with implementation details and would be harder to maintain and audit.
2. **No formal ratification.** Rejected because an unratified framework lacks decision authority, audit trail, and clear escalation path.
3. **Implementation-first approach.** Rejected because coding the engines before the governance layer was ratified would risk building non-compliant infrastructure.

---

## Risks and Mitigations

| Risk | Mitigation |
|------|------------|
| Volume text may require amendment as the ecosystem matures. | Amendment process is defined in BKGC Article XXXVII and BGS Section 15. |
| Future agents may not observe the Agent Oaths. | Agent registry entries require oath acceptance logging; non-compliant agents may be suspended. |
| Existing governance records may conflict with the new library. | Transition provisions in BKGC Article XXXIX require conflicts to be resolved under Article XIX and annotated accordingly. |

---

## Consequences

1. The five-volume library becomes the authoritative governance framework for the Blackstone Ecosystem.
2. All future knowledge objects, evidence evaluation, agent behavior, and compliance testing must conform to the library or document an approved exception via CDR.
3. Hermes Agent is authorized to act as the orchestration agent under the framework and is required to accept the Agent Oaths.
4. Volume II, III, IV, and V may be amended through the CDR process, but Volume I amendments require heightened governance board review.
