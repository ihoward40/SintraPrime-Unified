## Why This Matter Is Not Ready

### 1. No Primary Documents

The entire case currently rests on a fixture JSON file (`client_0001.json`) that contains structured data references — not actual documents. There are zero PDFs, zero screenshots, zero letters in the evidence folder. A dispute based on this would be:

> "I dispute this account because it's wrong."

That is not a legally sufficient dispute. Under FCRA § 611, a consumer must provide specific information about the inaccuracy. Without documents, there is no specificity.

### 2. No Contract

Without the Retail Installment Contract, we cannot:
- Verify the original loan amount
- Verify the APR and finance charges
- Determine whether the $12,517.55 balance is mathematically possible
- Identify the governing law provisions

### 3. No Repossession Paper Trail

Auto deficiency cases are won or lost on the UCC Article 9 paper trail. Without:
- Notice of Right to Cure
- Notice of Repossession (UCC § 9-611)
- Notice of Sale (UCC § 9-612/9-613)
- Sale results (UCC § 9-615)

...there is no way to evaluate whether UACC followed the law. And if they didn't follow the law, there is no way to prove it.

### 4. Prior CFPB Complaint Was Unsuccessful

UACC has already responded to a CFPB complaint and refused to delete the trade line. Filing another complaint without new evidence would likely produce the same result — and would weaken credibility with the CFPB.

### 5. No Dispute History

Without knowing what disputes have already been filed and what responses were received, we risk:
- Filing a dispute that has already been resolved
- Missing a FCRA § 611 reinvestigation failure
- Wasting time on issues already addressed
