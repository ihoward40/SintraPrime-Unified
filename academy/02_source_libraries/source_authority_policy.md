# Source Authority Policy

## Tier One — Controlling or Primary
Statutes, regulations, court opinions, official government guidance, governing instruments, contracts, filed records, audited financial records, official technical documentation.

## Tier Two — Professional or Persuasive
Treatises, practice guides, accounting standards, peer-reviewed research, recognized industry publications, bar and professional materials.

## Tier Three — Unverified or Lead-Generating
Videos, social posts, courses, templates, ebooks, forums, private manuals, promotional claims.

## Rules
- Tier Three sources cannot independently support a final legal, tax, accounting, medical, or financial conclusion.
- Every material claim must identify source tier, jurisdiction, effective date, and limitations.
- Conflicting Tier One sources must be surfaced, not hidden.
- Uploaded documents are evidence of what they say, not automatic proof that their legal conclusions are correct.
