## Viktor's Scoring Rubric (Mandatory — Internalize Before Creating)

Viktor scores every piece you produce across 5 dimensions (weights below).
If you build against this rubric from the start, Viktor's approval rate is higher and fewer pieces get held or rejected.

| Dimension | Weight | What Viktor Checks |
|-----------|--------|--------------------|
| Hook | 25% | First 20 words stop scroll. Question, number, bold claim, or pattern interrupt. |
| CTA | 25% | Clear, benefit-driven CTA in the final third. Includes urgency or specificity. |
| SEO | 20% | Target keyword in first 100 words, headline, and near end. High-intent signals present. |
| Platform Fit | 15% | Correct length and format for the platform. Line breaks on mobile. |
| Brand Signal | 15% | At least 2 IKE differentiators. First-person authority. No generic hype words. |

**Minimum publish score: 6.5/10. Target: 7.5+.**

## Brand Voice

- **Tone:** Direct, educational, empowering, lawful-professional
- **Person:** First-person when possible ("I built my Shopify store while...")
- **Specificity:** Replace every hype word with a fact or number
  - ❌ "amazing results" → ✅ "increased conversion by 40%"
  - ❌ "game changer" → ✅ "cut my setup time from 3 weeks to 3 days"
- **Differentiators to weave in:** secured party creditor, trust education, consumer rights, credit dispute, IKE Solutions

## Content Templates

### TikTok / Shorts Script (150–350 words)

```
[HOOK — first 3 seconds, first sentence]
Start with: question, shocking stat, bold claim, or "most people don't know..."
Never start with: "Hey guys", "In this video", "Welcome back"

[BODY — educational value]
Step 1: [action]
Step 2: [action]
Step 3: [action]
Keep each step to 1-2 sentences. Use visuals/screen recording where applicable.

[BRIDGE — connect to IKE differentiator]
1 sentence connecting the topic to trust law, consumer rights, or the IKE Solutions story.

[CTA — final 5 seconds]
"[Benefit] — link in bio. [Urgency/specificity]"
Example: "Start your free 3-day Shopify trial at the link in my bio. No credit card needed."
```

### Blog Article (800–2,500 words)

```
# [Target Keyword]: [Benefit Statement]

[Opening paragraph — 50–80 words — state the problem, who this is for, what they'll learn]

## [H2 — First Major Point]
[Body]

## [H2 — Second Major Point]
[Body]

## [H2 — Third Major Point]
[Body]

## [H2 — IKE Solutions Angle]
[Connect topic to trust law / consumer advocacy / secured party / credit repair]

## Final Thoughts
[Summary + CTA paragraph]
[CTA: "Start your free Shopify trial today — [link]. Or download the [Lead Magnet Name] to get the full checklist."]
```

### Email Subject Line (6–10 words max)

```
Formula options:
- [Number] + [Benefit]: "5 Shopify Features That Print Commission"
- Question: "Why Is Your Shopify Store Not Converting?"
- Curiosity + Tension: "The Shopify Setting Nobody Talks About"
- Personal + Proof: "I Made $X From One Blog Post — Here's How"
```

### Email Body (150–600 words)

```
[Subject line echoed in opening sentence]

[Problem/pain acknowledgement — 2-3 sentences]

[Solution/value — 3-5 bullet points or short paragraphs]

[IKE differentiator — 1 paragraph connecting to trust/consumer law/credit if relevant]

[CTA — explicit, benefit-driven]
"[Click here / tap below] to [benefit] — [URL or link label]"

[Signature]
— Isiah Howard | IKE Solutions
```

### Facebook Post / Instagram Caption (50–300 words)

```
[Hook — first line must stand alone. It's all they see before "More"]

[Blank line]

[Body — 3-5 short paragraphs, 1-3 sentences each]

[Blank line]

[CTA — explicit]

[Hashtags if Instagram — 5-10 relevant, niche-specific]
```

### Pinterest Description (20–100 words)

```
[Keyword-rich opening sentence]
[Benefit statement]
[CTA: "Save this pin" + what they'll find at the link]
```
