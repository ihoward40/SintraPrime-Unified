## The Problem

Most trust and administrative documents contain language that made sense
when they were created — but can trigger flags, rejections, or worse when
submitted to a bank, court, IRS, or credit bureau today.

We've seen:
- Certification of Trust documents with self-executing acceptance clauses
  that banks refuse on contact.
- Security agreements with fee-schedule language that creditors treat as
  threatening.
- Tax affidavits with non-resident framing that IRS agents flag immediately.
- UCC filings with accepted-for-value references that courts dismiss
  without reading.

**None of this means your trust is invalid.** It means the language needs
a compliance pass before it leaves your files.

---

## The Solution

The Trust Document Compliance Refactor runs your document through a
six-agent compliance engine:

1. **Intake** — We read every section and extract every relevant fact.
2. **Classification** — Every clause gets a GREEN, YELLOW, or RED rating.
3. **Rewrite** — YELLOW clauses are rewritten into bank-safe, court-safe language.
4. **Evidence Binder** — We organize your documents into a governed exhibit index.
5. **Recommendations** — We tell you exactly what you can do next, and where.
6. **Receipt** — Every run generates an immutable audit trail.

RED clauses are never sent anywhere. They are flagged for attorney or CPA
review before any external use.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Is this legal advice?**
No. This is a compliance classification and document review service.
We identify risk language and provide rewrites based on established
legal and regulatory standards. We always recommend attorney or CPA review
for RED-classified clauses before any external action.

**What kinds of documents can I submit?**
Certifications of Trust, Security Agreements, UCC Filings, Banking
Resolutions, Trustee Minutes, Tax Affidavits, and other administrative
trust documents.

**What happens if my document has RED clauses?**
RED clauses are blocked from all outputs. We flag them clearly, explain
why they are blocked, and refer you to an attorney or CPA for review.
You will still receive your run receipt and risk register.

**Can you submit documents to banks or courts on my behalf?**
No. This service produces compliance-reviewed documents and recommendations.
Submission to any external institution is always your decision and
responsibility, made with appropriate professional advice.
