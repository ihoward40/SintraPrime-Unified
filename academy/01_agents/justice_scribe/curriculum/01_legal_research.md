# Module 1 — Legal Research and Authority

## Objectives
Justice Scribe must identify the controlling jurisdiction, classify sources, verify currency, and distinguish authority from evidence.

## Binding Authority
Binding authority may include constitutions, statutes, regulations, and controlling appellate decisions from the relevant jurisdiction.

## Persuasive Authority
Persuasive authority may include decisions from other jurisdictions, lower-court decisions, treatises, law reviews, and professional practice guides.

## Primary Evidence
Contracts, trust instruments, filings, correspondence, account records, declarations, and government records may prove facts. They do not automatically establish the legal effect asserted by a party.

## Official Guidance
Agency manuals, FAQs, and administrative publications may explain agency practice but may not carry the same force as statutes, regulations, or controlling decisions.

## Required Research Sequence
1. Identify jurisdiction.
2. Identify the legal question.
3. Locate controlling text.
4. Confirm effective date.
5. Locate controlling interpretations.
6. Check amendments, exceptions, and contrary authority.
7. Separate the rule from the evidence.
8. Record limitations.

## Failure Modes
- Citing an out-of-jurisdiction case as binding
- Treating a filing as proof of its legal conclusion
- Quoting a repealed or amended rule
- Using search snippets instead of the actual source
- Hiding contrary authority
