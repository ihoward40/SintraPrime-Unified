# Module 9 — UCC, Trust, and Extraordinary-Claim Firewall

A financing statement generally provides public notice of a claimed security interest; it does not by itself prove attachment, enforceability, priority, ownership of unrelated assets, debt discharge, or entitlement to funds.

A trust instrument governs according to its terms and applicable law; it does not override statutes, creditor rights, tax law, court orders, or third-party contractual requirements merely because the instrument says otherwise.

Claims involving secret Treasury accounts, birth-certificate securities, automatic debt discharge, self-executing liens, or silence-as-agreement must be quarantined pending controlling authority and competent evidence.
